Okay, I'll be straight and frank.

Research is usually measured in terms of patents, papers.

Nope. It's not. Research comes in lot of steps. Professor writes project grants, sets up labs where usually the graduates and post graduates sit and explore, writes publications, reviews papers, talks at conferences, try to patent the tech, learn more and expand.

TL;DR, ELI5 line,

>"What new did you do to the world?"

..and IIIT-BH has research. It has done something. But when you ask how good or bad, we need to compare it with it's peers. ( let’s not! rather focus on how we can improve it. )

Every University of 30+ years has culture, has legends, has alumni, has experience driven faculty.

IIIT-BH is around ~10.
It has some culture, some great faculty and some brilliant people around to make the change, the push to the academic circle of human knowledge. 
Nah, not 'some', few.

The majority here strives to fill the day by the usual.

Leave home/hostel, take/attend classes, eat lunch, finish some administrative work and leave home for a nice warm supper and sleep.

The zeal for the interesting is present but soon it's overtaken by laziness.

Anyways, let's come to the point and look at the data.

As I'm from computer science I have targeted the computer science department only. ( folks from other branches, you can easily do this, extrapolate or fit the stats!)

For a university which totally focuses it’s human resource, manpower and wealth to placements, the research is quite satisfactory. The quality of research is another subject which is totally beyond the scope here.

Just a pro fact, **China.**

Chinese weren't quite great at publishing papers long back [20–30 years] but then slowly and then suddenly the graph peaked. China was publishing an astonishing amount of papers every year in all fields. This took the country from overlooked to well respected in academia. Quality? Not much. [ but in recent years, it’s very good ]

Sure it does need better faculty and maybe leave it’s old senile approaches which hinder the publication speed and quality but again, it’s new. It’s learning and like any neural net it takes time ( epochs ) to train for better accuracy. Let it run.
